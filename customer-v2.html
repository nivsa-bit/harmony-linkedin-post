I fired our AI call center vendor 90 days ago. Best decision I've made this year.

Here's why we left, and what happened next.

The problem wasn't the idea. It was the execution.

We hired 12 SDRs last year. By month 3, only 4 were performing. The rest? Still learning the product, still figuring out objections, still making the same mistakes their teammates had already solved. No knowledge sharing. No peer learning. Every rep was an island.

So we bought an AI call center. And it was worse.

The agents didn't learn. Week 1 and week 12 sounded identical. When one agent figured out a winning objection handling — the others never heard about it. We spent more time writing prompts than closing deals. And every time we asked "is it getting better?" the answer was "we need to retune it."

Then someone showed me Harmony.

What changed:

Setup took one afternoon. No prompt engineering. No scripting. We connected HubSpot, uploaded our call recordings, and Harmony built the agents from our actual winning calls — not from templates.

By week 2, I noticed something strange. One agent figured out that leading with a compliance angle closed 2x more in insurance. By week 3, every agent was doing it. Nobody told them to. They learned from each other.

By week 4, I ran my first "1:1" with an AI agent. I pulled up its performance, reviewed its calls, gave it feedback on pacing. It adjusted. Not next quarter. Right then. I managed it like I manage my human reps — and the interface made it feel natural, not technical.

Before we went live with a new vertical, Harmony ran our agents through hundreds of simulated calls against synthetic customer personas. Different industries, different objections, different moods. We knew exactly where they'd struggle before a single real prospect picked up.

Day 90 results:
Contact rate: 72% (was 38%)
Qualified meetings: up 61%
Prompts edited manually: 0
Agents that sound misaligned or off-brand: 0
Time spent "tuning" AI: 0 hours/week

The thing that surprised me most? Alignment. Every agent follows the same playbook, knows about our latest product release, uses the same proven techniques. It's like having a perfectly aligned team that never forgets a training session and never skips a team standup.

If you've been burned by AI call centers that promise the world and then need constant babysitting — the problem isn't AI. It's that nobody built it to actually learn.

Harmony did.
